The Buying Process

We found this bus on GovPlanet â€” a government surplus auction site. The listing had 11 photos and a brief mechanical inspection report. We drove 4 hours to inspect in person before bidding. Here's what we looked at on the inspection:

  • Started cold without excessive smoke
  • Drove smoothly â€” no slipping in the Allison transmission
  • No visible oil leaks under the engine compartment (engine is under a hatch inside the bus on a Type D)
  • Air brakes pressurize and hold properly
  • Pulled two sections of floor mat to inspect for rust â€” found surface rust only in the spots we checked (more on this later)
  • Body panels mostly solid, some minor dings on the rear corner
  • School district records showed oil changes every 10,000â€“12,000 miles, consistent coolant service

We bid on GovPlanet and won at $7,200 â€” about $2,000 under our maximum. Paid a $200 buyer's fee plus $180 to have it transported to our driveway on a flatbed (couldn't get it commercially insured instantly for driving). Total bus acquisition cost: $7,580.

Seat Removal: 72 Seats, 6 Hours

School bus seats bolt through the floor with two 1/2" bolts each. That's 144 bolts for 72 seats. Many were corroded and needed penetrating oil and an impact gun. We soaked them all the night before with PB Blaster. Total time: 6 hours for two people. The seats are heavy â€” budget for a way to move them. We stacked them outside and listed them on Facebook Marketplace. Sold 60 of them for $15 each. That's $900 back â€” not a bad offset.

Day 3

Floor Removal: The First Surprise

School buses typically have a plywood subfloor over the structural steel "frame" floor. Under the plywood is a rubber mat, and under the mat is bare steel. We pulled all the plywood panels (they're separate sections, not one piece â€” helpful) and found the rubber mat beneath in varying states of decomposition. The mat had been sealing moisture against the steel for decades in some areas.

When we pulled the rubber mat sections â€” that's where the surprises started.
