The Three Breakdowns

Breakdown #1: Flat Tire, Km 312

Pulled off to look at the Pacific view, drove back onto MEX 1, and immediately felt the rear driver's side go soft. Sharp rock on the shoulder had sliced the sidewall. Sidewall punctures can't be plugged â€” we were on the spare within 20 minutes and found a vulcanizadora (tire repair shop) in the next town, 34 miles south.

Resolution: $18 for a used matching tire mounted at the vulcanizadora. Took 25 minutes. Vulcanizadoras are everywhere in Baja and they are amazing.

Breakdown #2: Dead Battery in MulegÃ©, Km 1,102

Three straight overcast days meant our solar wasn't keeping up. We parked in MulegÃ© for two nights with high power usage (laptops, fridge, lights). Woke up to a battery at 4%. The engine battery (separate from house batteries) was fine â€” the starter van started. But we couldn't run anything until we found a shore power hookup for 4 hours.

Resolution: A small hotel let us plug in for $5. Lesson: in extended cloudy weather in Baja, your solar will underperform. Run your engine alternator to charge if needed, or have the shore power option available.

Breakdown #3: The Scary One â€” Overheating, Km 847

This was the one that could have ended the trip. Temperature gauge spiked suddenly near Loreto. We pulled over immediately â€” never drive through an overheating warning. Coolant reservoir was bone dry. The lower radiator hose had developed a small crack from vibration on the rougher sections of MEX 1. We lost maybe 2 liters of coolant in 20 minutes of driving before noticing.

Resolution: Let it cool for 2 hours. Used a hose clamp + duct tape over the crack as a temporary fix (it held for 300 miles). Bought a proper replacement hose and coolant in Loreto for $34 total. Mechanic labor: $20. The van has been fine since. Total downtime: 4 hours.

The Camping

Baja free camping is something else entirely. Beaches where you're the only vehicle for 5 miles in either direction. Desert nights so clear the Milky Way feels close enough to touch. Pacific sunsets that hit different when you're watching from the roof of a van on a cliff edge above the ocean.
