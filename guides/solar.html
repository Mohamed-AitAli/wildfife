Choosing the Right Panels

Monocrystalline vs Polycrystalline

Always go monocrystalline. They're more efficient (20‚Äì22% vs 15‚Äì17%), perform better in partial shade and high heat, and the price difference is now minimal. For a van roof where space is limited, efficiency matters enormously.

How Many Watts Do You Need?

A comfortable full-time setup typically needs between 400W and 800W of panels. Weekend warriors can get away with 200W. Budget for more than you think ‚Äî you can always add panels, but you can't add roof space.

MPPT vs PWM Charge Controllers

Always use an MPPT controller if your panels exceed 200W. MPPT is 25‚Äì30% more efficient in real-world conditions ‚Äî over a year, that's significant free energy. PWM is only appropriate for very small, simple setups.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistake Buying a PWM controller to save $40, then losing hundreds in energy every year. Buy MPPT once and never regret it.

Wiring: Do It Right or Do It Again

Undersized wiring causes fires. Use a proper wire gauge calculator. As a rule: run your main cables (battery to inverter) in 4/0 AWG or 2/0 AWG. Use an appropriately rated fuse within 18 inches of every battery terminal.

Essential Wiring Rules

  • Fuse within 18" of every positive battery terminal
  • Use tinned marine-grade wire ‚Äî it won't corrode
  • All connections crimped, not just twisted and taped
  • Run 12V DC and 120V AC wires in separate conduits
  • Install a battery monitor (Victron BMV-712 is the standard)
